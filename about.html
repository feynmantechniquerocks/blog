Do you know the guy in the picture ? 


He is Richard Feynman. He’s the world-known, nobel-prize-winning physicist.According to Physics World magazine, is one of the ten greatest physicist of all time.


But most importantly, he’s the name father of this blog. (Like he would have cared :D) 


Why I named this blog after him ? Because he’s a great explanier. He’s not only a legend physicist but also a great teacher. He was able to teach all the complex ideas in simple words.



When I was browsing through the learning methods, I came across with the Feynman Technique. I read about the technique and I really loved it and decided to apply it to topics I’d like to learn.This’s the reason why I started this blog. 


To apply Feynman Technique, there are 4 steps. 

  1. Choose a topic that you’d like to learn and study it.
  2. Teach the things you learn to someone or pretend that you’re teaching it.
  3. See the gaps in your explaination and go back and study about them more. 
  4. Once you think you understand the topic very well, review your understanding and simplify it. 

That's it. Feynman Technique is a simple yet an effective learning method that I'm going to use. I'll try to share simplified version of my understanding of various topics.
